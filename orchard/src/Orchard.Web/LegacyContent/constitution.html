Proposed ARA Constitution and Bylaws Changes

The current ARA constitution and bylaws were adopted in 1988 and have been amended on occasion since that time; the constitution was most recently amended in 1996. The board of directors of the ARA has enacted changes in the bylaws and recommends additional amendments to the constitution at this time. Pursuant to Articles VII and VIII of the ARA constitution, the ARA board would like to inform our members of the changes in the bylaws, and to call for a vote on the changes to the constitution.

The by-laws and constitution were examined by a subcommittee of the ARA board led by ARA past-president Kyle Frazier '83. Subcommittee members included Teddy Adams '91, Vicki Whamond Bretthauer '79, Tom Greene '71, Pat Huttenbach '92, Mary Lou Laubach '58, Gus Schill '55, and Dan Steiner '77; and, all ex officio, alumni director Mark Davis and staff members Rose Sundin and Ann Peterson '86.

The primary purposes of the changes are: to align the bylaws and constitution with how the ARA operates in the current university system; to balance the needs for governance and vision, with daily management; to better allow for proportional alumni representation; to allow the documents flexibility to keep pace with changing technology and communications media; and to exclude outdated or underutilized provisions.

In brief, changes to the bylaws and proposed changes to the constitution include:
 
 
Elimination of gender-specific language.
Elimination of the vice-president positions (2) and treasurer, and an increase in the number of board members from 18 to 21 to maintain current levels of representation.
Clarification of the definition of honorary and regular members.
An increase in the term of discretionary officers from two to three years. (Thus, anyone who serves a full term as an officer would be eligible for consideration as ARA president.)
Formalization of university authority over the alumni office budget, and of alumni office expenditures in accordance with university procedures.
Specification of the desired apportionment of Alumni Trustees as "two from the Houston metropolitan area and two from outside the Houston area, of which at least one should be from outside of Texas."
Formalization of the current ARA committee structure, in keeping with the organization's strategic plan, namely: Creating Community, Lifelong Learning, Encouraging Excellence, and Supporting Students.
Provision that the means by which the board may inform alumni members of meetings and other business may include traditional (mail, phone, fax) and other board-approved means (to provide for electronic communications and future technologies).

The ARA constitution requires an affirmative vote by association members for the changes to the constitution to take effect. The ARA board asks that all alumni cast their vote by mailing the ballot included in the Fall 2005 issue of the Owlmanac.
