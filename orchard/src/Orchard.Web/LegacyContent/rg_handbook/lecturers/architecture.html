Faculty Lecturers in Architecture

Clover Lee, Architecture

Assistant professor of architecture

Achievements: Principal of plusClover, a design firm based in LA and Houston; previously taught architecture at USC and UCLA
 

  • "Urban Black Hole: Agglomeration of Critical Masses in Urban China"
    		•

    In contrast to the dispersed model of horizontal development typical in North America, the hyper-dense, agglomerated (networked) cities of China emerge as a dynamic alternative model – one which can no longer be governed by master plans but instead needs to be redefined by constantly changing physical and economic forces. But with the speed of urban growth three times that of worldwide populations, China is rapidly approaching its threshold of critical mass – the boundary when an urban model entails a new state as it reaches its optimum size. This lecture will discuss how architecture design necessitates the conception of new building typologies and urban forms to accommodate this proliferation of urban expansion and densification.
