RICE ALUMNI DIGITAL LIBRARY
    		• I'm experiencing difficulty connecting to one of the Rice Alumni Digital Library resources. I get a blank page or a message about cookies being required.
    1. If you have pop-up blocking activated on your browser or through your Internet service provider, this must be deactivated as you work with the Digital Library resources.
    2. Confirm that you have your browser's Preferences menu set to accept computer cookies.
    3. Readers using Internet Explorer on the Macintosh experience more frequent difficulties with some Rice Alumni Digital Library resources. You may wish to try another browser, such as Netscape or Safari.
    4. Your personal or corporate firewall may be configured to block access to the Digital Library, which needs access to ports 80 and 443.
    		• I use AOL — can I use their browser?
    For best results, we recommend using Netscape or Internet Explorer for the ProQuest journal databases; ebrary also works well with Safari (Macintosh).
    		• I can't seem to view the books in the ebrary collection.
    Be sure you have downloaded and installed the ebrary Reader.™
    		• I can't seem to view the the "Page Image–PDF" fulltext of articles from the ProQuest article databases. Instead of the article text and images, I'm seeing just a blank window.
    1. Be sure to download and install the Adobe Reader® on your computer.
    2. At times, the proxy software we use to identify you as a valid user of these resources can interfere with Adobe Reader® and the opening PDF files (depending on your machine configuration and other software). We recommend:

      • Download the "Page Image–PDF" to your computer (generally either by clicking with the right mouse button instead of the left or by first pressing your control key before clicking on the link)
      • Double-click on the icon for the PDF file on your computer desktop — this will open the file directly in Adobe Reader®.
    		• I've used the Rice Alumni Digital Library resources before, but I'm having trouble now.
    It is possible that a garbled connection or transmission to one of these resources has disrupted the settings in your browser. We recommend:
    1. Clear the browser cache. (This is in the browser's Preferences settings, usually under "Advanced," and can be cleared by clicking on "Clear Now" or "Empty.")
    2. Delete your computer cookies. (This is in the browser's Preferences settings, usually under "Receiving Files" or "Privacy & Security")
    3. Restart your browser — that is, completely exit the browser and restart the program. Simply closing the browser window is usually insufficient.
    4. If these steps do not resolve the problem, please report the problem to us by email. We will work with Fondren Library, Rice IT, and commercial vendors to try and resolve the situation. Please provide the following, so that we may more quickly determine the source of the difficulty: which resource you were attempting to use and at what date/time, your computer type and browser type/version, at what point in connecting to the resource you received the error message and the exact text of the message.
