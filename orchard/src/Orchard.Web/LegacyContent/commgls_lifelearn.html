Committee Goals 2003–2004

Lifelong Learning

1) Hold Alumni College March 12–14, 2004 with a goal of 200 attendees.
   
  a) Have a one-day, reduced rate available on March 13 for young alumni with a goal of 40 attendees.
b) Have board members and Alumni College committee members introduce professors at Alumni College. Have ARA/committee members stress other ARA lifelong learning activities — Alumni College committee members should assign this task.
c) In the summer of 2004 make plans to include a lecture by a Rice professor in the 2005 Alumni College that will relate to a trip included in the 2005 Travel/Study program.
   
2) Hold a regional Alumni College (one day session) in New York City during the first half of 2004 with a goal of 5% attendance from population of alumni and parents.
  a) Staff select date in Spring 2004 that meets with their schedule. (Note that this could be after graduation.)
b) Recruit group in NYC to help with planning and suggested locations.
   
3) Hold more Back to Classes sessions during Homecoming 2003.
  a) Seven are planned versus four in 2002.
b) Have ARA board members and Alumni College committee members introduce professors at Homecoming. Have ARA/committee members stress other ARA Lifelong learning activities.
c) Have all volunteers stress Back to Classes at Homecoming information tent.
   
4) Create a committee to plan Alumni Leadership Conference for Fall 2005.
  a) Committee will be chaired by several board members. Other board members are encouraged to volunteer for the committee.
b) Actions will be to evaluate responses from attendees at 2002 conference as well as review and enhance the selection process for inviting alumni to attend.
c) Also plan what is to be presented. Add programs/volunteer needs for ARA as a highlighted event.
   
5) Lifelong Learning programs in key cities
  a) Staff should work with regional areas being stressed in Creating Community to ensure that appropriate faculty are available for events.
b) Work with staff to identify alumni in key cities who could also present at alumni events.
   
6) Staff enhancements to Lifelong Learning
  a) Explore web-learning possibilities. Goal is only to provide information to board during this year unless more can be accomplished.
b) Involve more faculty in Travel/Study program.
   
7) Longer term goals
  a) Tie in regional athletic event with travel program. Check 2004 football schedule for opportunities.
b) Work on joint lifelong learning possibilities with other universities in regional areas.
