Gay and Lesbian Alumni

We’ve come a long way in the last 20 years, but there’s much more ground to cover. As alumni, we are essential to the continued development of a harmonious campus environment. Our cohesion as a group and visibility as informed members of the Rice alumni network are essential to helping Rice’s future alumni have a better university experience.

While we’re most certainly an extremely diverse group of alumni, we share some core values; We’re proud of Rice, we’re proud of who we are, and we’re proud of the contribution GLBT students have made to the history of Rice as one of the nation’s foremost academic institutions.

GALA welcomes you as a member of one of the most dynamic alumni groups Rice has to offer.

Corey Devine ’03

A Welcome from Rice's President

Rice University is committed to providing a quality and supportive educational environment for all of our students. The opening in fall 2005 of GATHER, the new campus resource center to support Rice's GLBT student population, was an indication of our commitment. Many alumni responded favorably to this campus effort.

Rice's GLBT alumni — like alumni from many other institutions — have said that college was challenging enough without the added dimension of being gay or lesbian in a community that may not have always been supportive. Times are changing. It is my hope that through increased support for student initiatives, campus dialogue and programming, and with an active alumni organization, the university can continue to make progress.

The Rice community will continue to build an environment that supports our students — current and past — and prepares them for future success. Through this group, I hope that alumni will have the opportunity to enrich the lives of their peers and especially the lives of Rice's current students. Thank you for dedicating your time, energy, and resources to support Rice's GLBT community.

David W. Leebron
President, Rice University
