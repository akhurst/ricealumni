GIVE TO RICE

 

The Rice Ring symbolizes your treasured memories and the traditions of Rice University, the prestige of being a graduate of a world-class university, and a vital part of the distinguished Rice alumni network.

A recognizable statement of your pride and wisdom, your Rice Ring connects you to your fellow alumni, your university - past, present and future.

Beginning in the Spring 2013, Rice Ring recipients, distinguished alumni, and family members will be invited to a Rice Ring Celebration, a new tradition, to receive the Rice Rings. You will also be asked to turn your Rice Ring during your graduation ceremony, so that the University Crest faces outward, symbolizing that you have become an alumni and are ready for the world as a distinguished graduate of Rice University.

Add Your College Crest

During the 2011-2012 academic year, a student led panel formed to evaluate the Rice Ring Program - its significance historically and for the future. Consulting with the Association of Rice Alumni board, it was determined that the Rice Ring's symbolism should reflect the three significant connections for each student - connection to your graduating class, to your College, and to Rice University.

Beginning in September 2012, Rice Ring orders offer the option (not required) to add your College Crest or the Graduate Student Association's crest to the inside of the Rice Ring band.

History of
the Rice Ring

The Rice Ring has been the same traditional design for more than 94 years, and available exclusively from Balfour. The Centennial year of this great university adds to the Rice Ring's significance and the perpetuation of a rich historical tradition.

Eligibility

The Rice Ring is reserved exclusively for undergraduate students in good standing who have earned at least 90 credit hours, second-year Jones Graduate School students, and all other graduate students who have completed all requirements for their degree program.

All degreed Rice Alumni are also eligible.

Pricing and Payment Plan

Rice Ring prices vary widely and depend on the metal chosen and signet size. More information is available at www.balfour.com or consult a Balfour Representative at the Student Center Bookstore October 30 through November 1, 2012.
