GIVE TO RICE

 

Dear alumni,

     I know many of you are concerned about how Rice University fared during Hurricane Ike, and we have posted regular messages and updates on www.rice.edu to try to keep friends and families updated. In fact, with many of you living in the Houston and Galveston areas, we are concerned about your well-being. We hope that you and your families are safe and sound and were able to weather the storm with relatively little damage. To our alumni in Galveston and elsewhere along the nearby coast, we extend our deepest sympathy and hope that your lives and those of your neighbors return to normalcy as soon as possible.

     Let me tell you briefly about the situation at Rice. Beginning early last week, our crisis management team held regular meetings to prepare for the storm. We were already well prepared after experiencing an earlier hurricane drill and two significant storm scares thus far this season. Based on experiences with Hurricane Rita several years ago, we had a detailed plan in place that enabled us in a relatively brief period of time to prepare the campus for what turned out to be a fairly direct hit by a significant hurricane.

     We sheltered our undergraduate students in their colleges and designated certain extremely well-protected areas as shelters for the worst of the storm. All off-campus undergraduates were asked to come to their colleges for shelter prior to the storm. Graduate students living in Rice housing, or in areas subject to mandatory evacuation, were also sheltered on campus in McNair Hall and the Rice Memorial Center. Communication among the members of the crisis management team, and through them to the college masters and others managing the shelters, was maintained throughout the storm.

     I am happy to report that Rice came through the hurricane relatively unscathed. We had ample power, food and drinking water on campus. Water pressure following the storm posed a significant challenge, but that has returned to normal. Although a number of buildings suffered relatively minor damage, so far we know of none that cannot quickly be made functional. While the campus is littered with tree branches and a large number of smaller, newer trees were felled by the storm, we lost very few old large trees.

     Rice reopened on Tuesday, September 16, although with widespread power outages and road blockages throughout the city we understand the need to be flexible for faculty, staff and students who may have trouble getting to campus. We're aware that communication remains difficult for those without power.

     As I noted in my Web postings, I am grateful for the remarkable Rice staff who worked throughout this storm, sometimes at risk to their own safety and often apart from their own families, to assure that our students were safe and reasonably comfortable. Our students rose to the challenge as usual and worked with each other to make the hurricane experience as pleasant as it could be. We owe all of these individuals – the amazing Rice community – a debt of thanks for helping us come through the storm as well as we did.

     In sum, Rice University is in good shape. We can take pride in how our community has responded to this natural disaster. Ultimately, it will take weeks and perhaps more to return fully to all aspects of normal operations, but we expect to be quickly back in operation and able to carry on the teaching, research and service missions of the university. In the next few days, we will post more information about how Rice is assisting the greater community in its recovery efforts on the university’s primary Web site. Outreach efforts by our undergraduate students are being coordinated by Rice’s Community Involvement Center. The center is currently in project assessment discussions with local community organizations and will update its Web site, www.rice.edu/service, later in the week. In the meantime, we encourage the entire Rice community to assist neighbors, friends, colleagues, and fellow citizens as the Gulf Coast – and indeed, other communities along Ike's onward path – continues to recover from the hurricane.

     For those of you who may be planning to attend campus events this week, I encourage you to visit www.rice.edu or the Web site of the host department for updated information on the status of those events. For information on alumni activities, visit alumni.rice.edu. Please bear with us as not all departments will be operating at full capacity right away.

     As always, we thank you for your concern and support. And again, our thoughts are with those of you who also weathered and are dealing with the aftermath of the hurricane.

                                   Warm regards,

                                   David W. Leebron
                                   President, Rice University
