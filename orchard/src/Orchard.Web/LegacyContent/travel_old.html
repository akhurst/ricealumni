  • Panama Canal and Costa Rica
    		•

    March 8–17, 2007
    Starts at $3,795 per person plus airfare (based on double occupancy)
    Arrangements: Alumni Travel Group

    On this small-ship voyage, Rice travelers will discover nature's wonderland, Costa Rica, a country of breathtaking scenery—from seething volcanoes to impenetrable jungles—and home to more species than any other place on earth. Also on the itinerary is a transit of the Panama Canal from the Caribbean Sea to the Pacific Ocean, traversing three sets of locks and 90 years of history.

    Very few human endeavors have changed the face of the planet as did the Panama Canal. The canal shortened the passage from New York to San Francisco by almost 10,000 miles, spurring growth in developed countries as well as economic expansion in remote parts of the world.

    After arriving in Panama, you will board the 100-passenger Pacific Explorer in Colón on the Caribbean coast. Over the next 10 days, you will visit historic ports; take nature walks through tropical forests for close-up wildlife viewing; discover the incredible flora and fauna in this part of the world; and meet the Embera and Kuna Indians and learn about their customs, crafts, and dances. There also will be opportunities to relax, swim, snorkel, or kayak.

    Postcruise options are available for travelers who want to spend additional time in the tropics.
     
  • Village Life in the Dordogne
    		•
    April 12–20, 2007
    $2,495 per person plus airfare (based on double occupancy) by October 6, 2006
    $2,795 per person plus airfare (based on double occupancy) after October 6, 2006
    Arrangements: Thomas P. Gohagan & Co.

    This nine-day trip provides a unique opportunity to experience the authentic character of the historic villages in France's picturesque Dordogne, also known as Périgord Noir. You will explore prehistoric caves, medieval castles, and charming village squares, all while getting to know the culture and way of life of the residents of the region.

    Your home while in the Dordogne will be the family-owned Hôtel de la Madeleine located in Sarlat-la-Canéda, designated by the French government as one of the most beautiful villages in the country. A series of enriching lectures and guided excursions has been arranged to enhance your understanding and appreciation of this southwestern region of France. This includes visits to the legendary shrines of Rocamadour, the cave paintings and fossils in Les Eyzies-de-Tayac, the fortified village and castle of Beynac-et-Cazenac that was once a stronghold of Richard the Lionheart, and a private cruise on the tranquil Dordogne River aboard a traditional 19th-century gabare (barge).

    The cost of the trip includes hotel accommodations; three meals each day, including wine with dinner; and all sightseeing excursions. An optional two-day stay in Bordeaux is available at the beginning of the trip.
     
  • Waterways and Canals of Holland and Belgium
    		•
    April 23–May 1, 2007
    Starts at $1,795 per person plus airfare (based on double occupancy) by October 17, 2006
    Starts at $1,945 per person plus airfare (based on double occupancy) after October 17, 2006
    Arrangements: AHI International, Inc.

    There is no better way to experience the beauty, history, and culture of Holland and Belgium than by cruising their legendary waterways. Our itinerary begins in Brussels, Belgium, where you will embark the MS Switzerland II for an eight-day, seven-night cruise that ends in Amsterdam, the capital city of the Netherlands. Some of your excursions along the way will include visits to Antwerp, one of Europe's leading art centers; picturesque Bruges, located in the heart of Flemish Belgium; The Hague, seat of the Dutch government; the historic city of Delft, famous for blue-and-white porcelain and painter Johannes Vermeer; and the world-renowned floral extravaganza known as Keukenhof Gardens. Your understanding of this colorful and intriguing corner of Europe will be enhanced by informative educational programs and excursions that range from a lecture on the painters of Holland's Golden Age to a visit to the Delta Project, the fascinating engineering project that protects Holland from the sea.

    All cabins onboard the 102-passenger MS Switzerland II face outward and afford sweeping views of the passing panoramas. They are all air-conditioned and feature a television, radio, safe, minibar, hair dryer, and a private, modern bathroom.
     
  • Treasures of Southern Africa
    		•
    May 21–June 3, 2007
    $5,095 per person (based on double occupancy) by October 3, 2006
    $5,295 per person (based on double occupancy) after October 3, 2006
    Plus approximately $1,400 round-trip airfare from Washington, D.C.
    Arrangements: AHI International, Inc.

    Southern Africa is a land of spectacular biological and cultural diversity. This thoughtfully designed itinerary features the very best of South Africa and southern Zambia. The elegant Mount Nelson Hotel in Cape Town, South Africa, will be your home for the first four nights. Over the next few days, you will have time to experience this beautiful city; to explore the UNESCO World Heritage Site, Robben Island, the infamous political prison that housed Nelson Mandela; and to travel to Cape Peninsula, home to a spectacular display of plant life that represents nearly 20 percent of the African continent's flora. You also will spend two nights in Livingstone, Zambia, at the colonial-style Royal Livingstone Hotel and will have free time to visit magnificent Victoria Falls. While in modern Johannesburg, South Africa, travelers will visit historic Soweto and tour the Apartheid Museum. The last part of the itinerary is the safari experience: three nights at Thornybush Private Game Reserve, overlooking a scenic savannah and located adjacent to Kruger National Park.

    Throughout this journey, travelers will have preferred access to popular attractions and specially arranged cultural experiences and will be treated to sumptuous cuisine and stay in luxurious five-star accommodations that have played host to luminaries and statesmen.
     
  • Great Rivers and Waterways of Europe
    		•
    June 29–July 14, 2007
    From approximately $4,495 per person plus airfare (based on double occupancy)
    Arrangements: INTRAV

    On this summertime journey, you will wind your way through Europe on a network of rivers from Amsterdam to Budapest and experience a lush landscape dotted with little-known towns and villages, as well as premier historic cities that make this part of the world so enticing. You will see storybook castles on the hillsides of the Rhine River region, visit sleepy medieval towns that border the Main River, marvel at the Rhine-Main-Danube Canal, and experience the fairytale quality that is evident along the Danube.

    Ports of call will include Cologne, the Rhine River's largest city and fourth-largest in Germany; Miltenberg, known for its architectural treasures, timber-framed houses, and wine cellars dating from 1541; ancient Bamberg, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, dating from 902; Nuremberg, one of Bavaria's loveliest cities, famous as the site of the 1946 War Crime Trials; the 2,000-year-old Bavarian village of Passau, where colorful houses line the banks and green pastures rise into the countryside; and the elegant Habsburg capital of Vienna.

    The new 128-passenger Sound of Music, the most ultradeluxe five-star vessel sailing Europe's waterways, will be your home during this journey. All sightseeing and shore excursions are included in the price of the trip.
     
  • Village Life in the Italian Lake District
    		•
    July 21–29, 2007
    Approximately $2,595 per person plus airfare (based on double occupancy)
    Arrangements: Thomas P. Gohagan & Co.

    Enjoy a nine-day sojourn in Italy's romantic Lake District, where splendid alpine peaks tower over pristine lakes surrounded by picturesque villages, tropical orchards, and grand Renaissance palaces.

    Travelers will spend seven nights in the charming four-star Le Due Corti Hotel in the heart of medieval Como. The hotel is an old post house for passing travelers that was fully restored in 1992 and combines modern amenities and comforts with the ambiance of its late 19th-century Italian decor and furnishings. A deluxe continental breakfast and dinner each evening is included.

    A Village Life program, this trip includes a full array of educational and cultural enrichment, featuring tours and lectures. Among the specially arranged excursions are an early opening tour at the legendary Villa Balbianello; a private cruise on Lake Como to scenic Bellagio; a spectacular, full-day tour of Lake Maggiore and a cruise to the Borromean Islands with visits to Isola Bella and Stresa; a curator-led tour of the Silk Museum, chronicling Como's silk industry; a culinary demonstration at the Centro Studi Alberghiero Casnati cooking school; and an exclusive tour at the Aldo Galli Academy of Fine Arts, which specializes in restoration of historic art.
     
  • Treasures of Scandinavia and Russia
    		•
    August 9–22, 2007
    From approximately $1,899 per person plus airfare (based on double occupancy)
    Arrangements: CruiseCenter

    Join Rice alumni and friends on this 12-day cruise aboard Holland America's Rotterdam VI from Copenhagen, Denmark, to London, England. The Rotterdam VI is a classic cruise ship that features state-of-the-art amenities, including a world-class spa and fitness center, Internet access, and spacious, elegantly appointed staterooms, many with private verandahs.

    Ports of call during this cruise are Tallinn, the capital city of Estonia, with a well-preserved medieval old town; St. Petersburg, Russia's treasure trove of culture and history; cosmopolitan Helsinki, set along the beautiful Gulf of Finland; Stockholm, Sweden, one of the world's most beautiful capitals as well as one of Europe's leading economic regions; Warnemunde, Germany, once a quaint fishing village and now an important German shipping center; Arhus, Denmark, founded more than 1,000 years ago as a Viking settlement; and Oslo, Norway, one of the oldest Scandinavian capitals, beautifully situated at the head of the Oslo Fjord.

    Travelers are free to explore on their own or take advantage of a program of optional excursions, offered at an additional cost, that delve more deeply into the history, culture, and natural beauty of each port. Optional extensions also are available for those who want to spend time in Copenhagen and London.
     
  • Ottoman Empires
    		•
    September 1–14, 2007
    From approximately $2,999 per person (based on double occupancy)
    Free round-trip air from selected cities, including Houston
    Arrangements: Alumni Travel Group

    The itinerary for this fascinating trip begins in Istanbul, Turkey, a city considered to be a paradise for antiquities lovers. Other ports of call are Nessebur, Bulgaria, one of the oldest towns in Europe; Constanta, Romania, named after the Roman emperor Constantine, who fortified and developed the city; Odessa, Ukraine, named one of 12 Soviet "Hero Cities" for valor during WW II; historic Sevastopol, Ukraine, which boasts an ancient Greek theater and lovely architectural monuments; Sochi, Russia's leading vacation resort and the site of Stalin's dacha (summer house); Yalta, Ukraine, the site of the summer palaces of Tsars Alexander III and Nicholas II; and Kusadasi, Turkey, gateway to magical Ephesus ruins. Spectacular Santorini, Greece, will be the final port before disembarking in Athens.

    Your ship will be the Oceania MS Nautica, an upscale 684-passenger ship. Known for fine cuisine at sea, its gourmet culinary program was created by world-renowned master chef Jacques Pépin. The majority of staterooms and suites feature private, teak verandas.

    Travelers are free to explore on their own or take advantage of a program of optional excursions, offered at an additional cost, that delve more deeply into the history, culture, and natural beauty of each port. Pre- and postcruise extensions are available for travelers who want to spend additional time in Istanbul or Athens.
     
  • Campus Abroad in Peru
    		•
    September 20–27, 2007
    Approximately $2,095 per person plus airfare (based on double occupancy)
    Arrangements: AHI International

    From the misty, mystical hush of the once lost city of Machu Picchu to ancient Cuzco, clinging to its perch high in the Andes, to the cosmopolitan colonial flair of Lima, Peru displays the diversity of the ancient and modern cultures that shaped this extraordinary country. Travelers will explore the historic heart of Lima, including one of the largest museums in South America, the National Museum of Archaeology, Anthropology, and History; visit Cuzco, the sacred city of the Incas and the archaeological capital of the Americas; the ancient fortress of Ollantaytambo and the small traditional village of Chinchero that overlooks the Sacred Valley of the Incas; and finally, the UNESCO-recognized antiquity Machu Picchu, a complex of nearly 200 houses, palaces, and temples.

    This is one of our popular Campus Abroad programs and is characterized by its emphasis on education. During the trip, a series of informative educational programs will be presented by local experts to enhance your insight into the region. As past participants of our Campus Abroad programs know, this is an all-inclusive program with accommodations, three full meals each day, and tours led by knowledgeable guides included in the price of the trip. Flights between Lima and Cuzco also are included.

  • Bavarian Markets
    		•
    December 4–12, 2007
    Approximately $2,295 per person plus airfare (based on double occupancy)
    Arrangements: AHI International

    Our final trip of 2007 is a wonderful way to escape the frantic pace and pressures of the holiday season at home by experiencing the European Advent spirit in the heart of Tannenbaum territory. Travelers will spend three nights in the charming walled city of Nuremberg and four nights in Munich, the glittering capital of Bavaria, with included tours and excursions in each city. You will explore the best of Germany's famed Christkindlmarkts, including its most famous in Nuremberg. You will travel Germany's famous Romantic Road; visit King Ludwig II's final castle, Schloss Herrenchiemsee, designed to be a replica of Versailles; see a unique "living crib" nativity scene at Kloster Andechs, a historic hilltop monastery; and savor a delicious dinner amidst the quiet beauty of the Bavarian countryside at a traditional German inn.

    Imagine yourself sipping a mug of hot-mulled wine or warm spiced cider as you browse ornate vendor stalls festooned with fragrant pine branches and bright boughs of holly. Also, imagine the crisp air redolent with the aromas of gingerbread, cinnamon, fresh-baked apple strudel, grilled sausages, and other enticing seasonal treats, and join fellow alumni and friends for a very special year-end trip.
