Association of Rice University Black Alumni (ARUBA)

Board Member Responsibilities

In order to carry out its mission to build a reciprocally beneficial relationship between the Black alumni and the Rice University community, the ARUBA board should consist of members who enthusiastically advocate and support ARUBA as well as Rice University activities as well as take an active role in the life of the university.  While the board understands that its members will have varied interests and strengths, all members are expected to meet the standards of participation outlined in this document.

Relating to the ARUBA Board and its Committees

  • The ARUBA board will meet two times a year: typically once (physically) in the fall during Homecoming and spring (either by conference call or physically during Graduation weekend).  Board members commit to attend all board meetings each year, subject only to unforeseeable exigencies. 
  • Board members commit to serve on an ARUBA standing committee (such as Alumni Relations, Nominations, or Communications) or other board committee.  There should be little extra burden to non-Houstonians in serving on these committees, which make use of e-mail and telephone conferences.

Relating to ARUBA Programs and Activities

  • The ARUBA’s signature events include Homecoming & Reunion and the Rice Black Alumni Recognition event.  Board members commit to attend Homecoming and the ARUBA general meeting and event every year . Expenses related to attending Homecoming and other Rice or ARUBA events are the responsibility of individual board members.
  • Board members commit to attend any ARUBA or Rice event in their area especially those involving the Rice president to which they are invited, subject only to unforeseeable exigencies, and to serve on ARUBA host and other event committees if asked.
  • Board members who live outside of Houston commit to take an active role in local alumni Rice and ARUBA activities and in promoting ARUBA and Rice involvement to fellow black alumni.
  • Board members commit to being active between ARUBA meetings by responding to ARUBA related e-mails and correspondence and participating in e-mail and conference call discussions and votes.
  • Board members should take advantage of some of the many opportunities to get involved in the life of the university.  Examples include joining the Rice Alumni Volunteers for Admission, involvement as a community associate at one of the colleges if a Houston-area resident, participating in the online Rice Career Network, work on fundraising campaigns, or any one of dozens of other similar opportunities.  A more comprehensive list of volunteer opportunities at Rice can be found in the “Volunteer” section of the Alumni Affairs web site.

Giving to Rice

  • Recognizing the importance of alumni giving in general, the ARUBA board of directors shall make a commitment to donate every year to Rice University in some capacity (i.e. Rice Annual Fund, Black Students Association, Rice NSBE chapter or other designated interest at Rice giving).  This can be for any amount suitable to one’s circumstances.
