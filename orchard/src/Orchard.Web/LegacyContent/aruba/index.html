Association of Rice University Black Alumni (ARUBA)

Mission

ARUBA seeks to provide a forum for black former students of Rice University to develop and/or maintain relationships with each other and with the Rice University community as a whole- students, parents, faculty and staff. ARUBA strives to achieve this objective by stimulating intellectual and social participation, encouraging contributions to the university's enhancement for diversity and strengthening the communications as well as the bond of understanding between the university and its black alumni community.

Strategic Objectives

  • Maintaining direct contacts to current and future students to assess ways in which alumni can provide direct guidance or assistance with assimilation into campus life and facilitating opportunities to enhance the Rice academic experience.
  • Providing networking and career development opportunities for minority alumni by capitalizing on resources within the organization.
  • Fostering positive, productive, and effective relationships between ARUBA and the University, its leadership, faculty/staff and appropriate stakeholders.
  • Developing and maintaining all external and internal mass communications for Rice Black Alumni. This includes content for the ARUBA ARA Page content, Rice publications, ARUBA website, Facebook page, Yahoo Group listserv, and LinkedIn page. TBD: Twitter, Rice listserv ARUBA-L

To stay up-to-date electronically on ARUBA news and events, please bookmark this page and join our email broadcast list.

For discussion, join our Yahoo! group, arubaonline.

If you have any questions or comments, please feel free to contact ARUBA Chair, Angela Berry Roberson '90 or ARUBA Communications Chair, Kenitra Brown '07.

The Blueprint for Excellence Awards

Awards for nominations are:

  • Legacy Award - Honorees

    • Chandler Davidson, Rice Professor Emeritus, Department of Sociology, and Catherine "Cathi" Clack, Director Multicultural Affairs and Assistant Dean for Student Life, are the recipients of ARUBA’s 2012 Legacy Award, which recognizes  Rice alumni, faculty, staff or friends who have advanced the interests and needs of Black students, faculty, and Alumni at Rice through exceptional dedication and commitment.

  • Outstanding Achievement in Career - Honorees

    • Two ARUBA Rice graduates — Herbert Anderson, '03 Jones School, NASA Project Manager and Michael Wilks '01, Professional Basketball Player/ Entrepreneur — will receive the 2012 Award for Outstanding Achievement in Career, presented to alumni who have advanced the interests and standards of excellence of Rice through distinctive professional careers.

  • Outstanding Achievement in Service/Contributions to the University - Honorees

    • Terrence Gee '86, Trilogee Venture Partners, Managing Partner, will receive the 2012 ARUBA Award for Outstanding  Achievement in Contributions and Service to the University, which recognizes those who have made significant, sustained and voluntary contributions of energy, time and creativity toward the advancement of the university.

  • Outstanding Achievement in Civic and Community Service - Honorees

    • Pamela Scott '73, LVCC Inc., President & CEO, will receive the 2012 Outstanding Alumni Award for Achievement in Civic and Community Service, presented to alumni who have advanced the interests and standards of excellence of Rice through distinctive service in their community

For more information about the nominations process, please contact LaCresha Lamb in the Rice Alumni Office at llamb@rice.edu.
