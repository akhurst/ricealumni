Rice Business Network

The Rice Business Network (RBN) is dedicated to helping members of the Rice community succeed in life and business through personal development, professional development, and interpersonal connections.

Annual Networking Event ::
"30 Seconds of Fame.... Mastering Your Elevator Speech!"
September 9, 2009 • Gallery M Squared
339 West 19th Street

6:30 p.m. Reception
7:00 p.m. Program
Catered by Shade in the Heights

We’ve all been to myriad networking events where we stand around, maybe meet some people who are maybe useful to know, and leave feeling like maybe something has been accomplished. This event is NOT one of those!

We will put networking concepts into practice with coaches on hand to offer feedback. Each attendee will be randomly assigned to a table, where he/she will be given 30 seconds to deliver an elevator pitch. After every attendee has presented, time will be allocated for group follow-ups, then participants will change tables and start over again. Each table will feature a coach taking notes on elevator pitch delivery and follow-up networking. At the end of the night, attendees will receive feedback from both the coaches and the other attendees at their tables. Come hone your networking skills with fellow alumni!

About the RBN

What does the Rice Business Network (RBN) do?
The Rice Business Network coordinates events for Rice people to work together and help each other with business challenges and issues. It has helped members of the group address a wide variety of issues including finding employees, improving sales, starting businesses, making investments, buying and selling real estate, fixing computer problems, and handling accounting and legal issues. The group has been meeting regularly for more than six years.

As mentioned above, the RBN meets the second Friday of each month for a networking lunch. Occasionally, the RBN will host a forum or in-depth discussion on a particular industry or business issue. E-mail the RBN if you have a topic of interest or if you would like to lead a discussion.

Who attends the Rice Business Network meetings?
A wide variety of Rice people attend the RBN's informal roundtable meetings. Many regular attendees of the meetings are entrepreneurs or self-employed, while others have years of corporate experience. Everyone in these get-togethers finds a supportive group and can enjoy the wide-ranging discussions and ideas.

The RBN Board
The RBN Board consists of members of the Rice community who have volunteered to coordinate events and perform the administrative tasks necessary to sustain the organization. Board members serve one-year terms that currently run through June 30, 2008.
