ARA Laureates Program

Established in 1937, the Association of Rice Alumni's honors program recognizes the contributions and accomplishments of outstanding Rice alumni and friends of the university.

ARA Awards
Nominations are accepted each year for three awards: the Gold Medal, the Distinguished Alumni Award, and the Meritorious Service Award. The selection committee may award one or more of each award each year. Completed nominations for 2009 candidates are due August 1, 2008.

2007 Distinguished Alumni Awards

  • Bill Arhos '57
    		•

    Working for KLRU-TV, a Public Broadcasting Service station serving Austin and San Antonio, Bill Arhos produced a pilot program in 1974 featuring Willie Nelson for a PBS music television series. That series was Austin City Limits. Now in its 31st consecutive season on PBS, Austin City Limits is seen throughout the U.S. and beyond. Arhos served as executive producer for the series through 1999. He also served as executive producer for more than 15 television music specials with PBS. Arhos has been elected to the PBS board of directors twice, and elected to serve on the board of directors for the Country Music Association. Upon his retirement, KLRU-TV established an endowment in his name. The 2003 National Endowment for the Arts Gold Award was presented to Bill Arhos by President Bush, marking the first time the award was given for the founding of a television series.
     
  • James H. Newman '82, '84
    		•

    James Newman received a Bachelor of Arts degree in physics from Dartmouth College in 1978 and came to Rice for his masters and doctorate degrees in physics, awarded in 1982 and 1984, respectively. After staying at Rice an additional year for post-doctoral research, he was appointed adjunct professor in the Department of Physics and Astronomy in 1985. That same year he began work at NASA's Johnson Space Center, where his duties included responsibility for conducting flight crew and flight control team training for all mission phases in the areas of Orbiter propulsion, guidance and control. In 1988 Newman was award the NASA Superior Achievement Award, and he entered astronaut training in July of 1990. A veteran of four space flights, he has logged more than 43 days in space, including six spacewalks. In 2002 Newman was detailed to the International Space Station Program Office, serving as NASA's Director, Human Space Program, Russia. In this role he oversaw all human space flight operations, logistics, and technical functions, including NASA's mission operations and crew training in Russia.
     
  • Arthur L. Storey Jr. '59
    		•

    Arthur Storey graduated from Rice in 1959 with a degree in English. As a Distinguished Military Graduate he entered the U.S. Army as a second lieutenant and served in the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers. After completing his military service, Storey returned to Texas and received a degree in civil engineering from Lamar Tech University. He spent the next 24 years working for three private engineering firms in Houston on a variety of municipal engineering projects. He served on and chaired the Houston Chamber of Commerce's flood control and drainage committee. In 1989 he was named director of the Harris County Flood Control District. He brought leadership and unity to all of the county's infrastructure programs, including roadways, draining, parks, libraries, the extensive county building program, and the Harris County Toll Road Authority. He is widely respected in the engineering profession for his imagination as well as his effectiveness and fairness in his administration of the complex infrastructure issues faced by Harris County. In 1997 Harris County Commissioners Court honored his work by naming a 250-acre flood control facility and county park adjacent to Brays Bayou " Arthur Storey Park."
     
  • George P. Taylor Jr. '75
    		•

    George Taylor received his Bachelor of Arts degree in physics and Russian language from Rice in 1975. He was commissioned a second lieutenant in the Air Force Reserve through the Health Professions Scholarship Program. Following his graduation from Baylor College of Medicine in 1978 and subsequent internship, Taylor entered active duty in 1979. Over the last 28 years, he has held more than 14 appointments in Japan, Spain, Nevada, California, and Utah. He as been in Washington, D.C., since 1995, when was named chief of the Aerospace Medicine Division. In 2002 he was named Surgeon General of the Air Force, a position he held until his retirement from the Air Force in 2006.
     
  • James S. Turley '77, '78
    		•

    James Turley began his career with Ernst & Young in 1977 in the firm's Houston office. Over the last 28 years he has held a series of leadership positions throughout Ernst & Young. He was named metropolitan New York area managing partner of Ernst & Young in 1998 and was appointed deputy chairman in 2000. In July 2001 he became chairman and assumed the role of CEO in October, 2003. Based in New York and London, Turley serves as senior advisory partner for many of Ernst & Young's largest global clients. Turley also recently developed a program called "People First," which focuses on demonstrating that the more than 80,000 employees of Ernst & Young are valued at the highest levels.


    This award is presented to alumni who have advanced the interests and standards of excellence of Rice University through distinctive professional or volunteer careers. It is reserved for those who attended the university as an undergraduate or graduate student for a period of at least one year and are living at the time of consideration. No one who has received the Gold Medal is eligible for this award.

    2007 Meritorious Service Awards

  • Martha Lou Broussard '57
    		•

    Martha Lou Broussard has shown decades of dedication to the students and faculty of Rice's Department of Earth Sciences. She was the first woman to graduate from what was then the Rice geology department and after a successful career in the oil industry she joined the department in 1983 as department coordinator. Martha Lou officially retired from Rice in 1989, but has been the volunteer alumni coordinator since that time. She organizes events across the country when alumni gather for professional conferences, has organized two festivals in honor of retiring faculty that attracted alumni and other geologists from around the world to come to Rice, was responsible for many of the arrangements for the 50th anniversary celebration of the Earth Sciences department, plus smaller events each year. Every year Mary Lou does countless jobs that help keep countless alumni connected to Rice and to each other.
     
  • Katherine B. Dobelman '46
    		•

    Kay Dobelman has been a staunch supporter of Rice for more than 30 years and has served in many capacities during that time. As president of Friends of Fondren, she began to include men on the board and create a more inclusive volunteer body. She has continued to be a strong advocate for Fondren Library, served a term as an Association of Rice Alumni board member, served as a member of the ARA honors committee, the major gifts committee, and Golden Class Scholarship Committee. She has also been active in the Shepherd Society, serving a term on the governing council and as secretary. She also worked on the Jones Graduate School capital campaign, a part of the most recent university campaign, to fundraise $65M for JGSM construction and growth.
     
  • Cathryn Rodd Selman '78
    		•

    Cathryn has been a regular and long-standing presence on campus for many years. She has served on the major gifts committee and the university standing committee for undergraduate curriculum. She was the chair of the 25th reunion campaign for her class in 2003 and served as chair of the Annual Fund. She has served two terms as a member of the board of the Friends of Fondren Library. Cathryn has been a sponsor of the celebration of the Rice Women and Gender Studies Program and has also been active in planning both the 2002 Alumni Leadership Conference and the conference in 2005.
     
  • Richard O. Wilson '52, '56 and Mary Ellen Wilson '54
    		•

    As individuals, Dick and Mary Ellen Wilson have contributed their time again and again, and as a couple they have given of their time and means to Rice with enthusiasm and generosity. Dick has been an active member of the Rice Engineering Alumni and is the organization's current president. He was chair of the 45th and 50th reunion committees for his class and class giving chair for the Annual Fund. Mary Ellen served on the Class of 1954 Golden Anniversary Scholarship committee and worked on her 25, 35, 40 and 45-year reunions. She also has served a term as 1954 class giving chair for the Annual Fund. Dick and Mary Ellen were honored by the Friends of Fondren Library in 1991. They are also members of the Shepherd Society and Rice Historical Society.


    This award was established in 1986 to provide recognition for those who have made significant, sustained, and voluntary contributions of energy, time, and creativity toward the advancement of the university. Alumni, active or retired faculty, staff, administrators, and friends of the university who are living at the time of their selection are eligible. No one who has received the Gold Medal is eligible for this award.

    2007 Gold Medal

  • C. Sidney Burrus '57, '58, '60, and Mary Lee Burrus
    		•

    Mary Lee and Sidney Burrus's great partnership has enabled them to loyally serve Rice for more than 40 years. As masters of Lovett College, they had a tremendous influence on many students, guiding them through their college experience by setting the tone and serving as role models. They have been ambassadors for the university at many Rice events including hosting groups in their home and accompanying alumni and friends of Rice as part of the Rice Alumni Travel/Study Program. The alumni office employed Mary Lee in the 1990s, taking advantage of her planning skills to coordinate presidential visit to cities across the country. Sidney has guided countless electrical engineering students in teaching and research, as well as administration of the George R. Brown School of Engineering. His professional recognition has advanced the academic excellence of the university and his numerous teaching awards are proof of his dedication to Rice students.


    This is the highest award presented by the Association of Rice Alumni. The Gold Medal is designed to recognize Rice alumni, faculty, staff, or friends who have rendered extraordinary service to the university. Each recipient must demonstrate one or more of the following criteria: outstanding service in promoting the ideals of the founder of the university OR unusually deep dedication to and advancement of the academic excellence of the university OR uncommon generosity of time and means in support of the university. Preference will be given to living nominees, but posthumous awards are not forbidden.
