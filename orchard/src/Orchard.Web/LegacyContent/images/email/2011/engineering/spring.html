It has been a busy, interesting and at times, delightful year for me as interim dean of the George R. Brown School of Engineering. Although I have thoroughly enjoyed getting to know the workings of the School once again, it will be with great pleasure that I hand over the reins to Dean Ned Thomas on July 1. But before I relinquish my position, I’d like to say that one of the most gratifying things about the past year has been to get to know our stellar junior faculty. This year, three of them have received National Science Foundation CAREER Awards and you can read about them in this issue of the Rice Engineering Newsletter. You can also read about P.M. Ajayan winning not one but two MURIs. He’s the only person in the country to do so and this distinction speaks volumes about his research program.

Another source of pleasure has been our undergraduate students and the work they’ve been doing in the Oshman Engineering Design Kitchen. Engineering design has entered a new phase since the opening of the OEDK. The sophistication and complexity of undergraduate design at Rice is fed by our industrial partners and those in the Texas Medical Center and nurtured by the faculty who direct the projects. Not to be overshadowed are the many students who won NSF Graduate Research Fellowships. Rice has a good track record of bringing in these awards and this year was no exception.

I hope you enjoy reading about our new dean and the accomplishments of our faculty and staff. For more news from the School, visit our webpage at http://engr.rice.edu.

Sidney
C. Sidney Burrus
William and Stephanie Sick Dean of Engineering, Interim

Ned Thomas appointed Dean of Engineering

Edwin L. "Ned" Thomas, current head of the Department of Materials Science at MIT, has been named the William and Stephanie Sick Dean of Engineering, effective July 1, 2011.

Saterbak's impact as educator
acknowledged with top teaching prize

Ann Saterbak, professor of the practice in bioengineering, took the George R. Brown Prize for Excellence in Teaching, Rice's top annual teaching award.

Three win NSF CAREER Awards

Jeffrey Jacot, bioengineering; Jamie Padgett, civil and environmental engineering; and Lin Zhong, electrical and computer engineering have won National Science Foundation CAREER Awards for young faculty.

Ajayan wins two MURIs

P.M. Ajayan, Benjamin M. and Mary Greenwood Anderson Professor in Engineering Materials Science and Nanotechnology, is the only recipient of two Department of Defense Multidisciplinary University Research Initiative grants for 2011.

Design projects define senior year for engineers

Seniors are tackling ever more complex design projects, thanks to the resources of the Oshman Engineering Design Kitchen, design advisors on and off campus and students' desire to make a difference in peoples' lives.

Graduate students, seniors win federally
funded graduate research fellowships

Clayton Shepard, graduate student in electrical and computer engineering has won the prestigious National Defense Science and Engineering Graduate Fellowship and eight graduate and undergraduate students have won the National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellowship grants for 2011.
