Social Sciences Alumni Newsletter Spring 2012


 

Rice receives $25 million naming gift from alumnus Robert Klein for a new social sciences building
Rice University has received a $25 million gift from alumnus Robert Klein to name a new School of Social Sciences building. The Rice Board of Trustees approved the proposal at its March 22 meeting. The Robert A. Klein Hall for Social Sciences will house the majority of the school’s current academic departments, institutes and centers. It will be built on campus near the James A. Baker III Institute for Public Policy and Jesse H. Jones School of Graduate Business.

 

  $2 million grant from Templeton World Charity Foundation to support Rice study of science and religion around the world
A $2 million grant from the Templeton World Charity Foundation will enable sociology professor Elaine Howard Ecklund to conduct the first cross-national study of how scientists around the world view religion and science.

  
   
Hand counts of votes may cause errors
In the study “Post-Election Auditing: Effects of Election Procedure and Ballot Type on Manual Counting Accuracy, Efficiency and Auditor Satisfaction and Confidence,” psychologist Michael Byrne found that hand counting of votes in postelection audit or recount procedures can result in error rates of up to 2 percent.
   
Economist's paper calls for alternative to Hormuz oil route
Improvements to the pipelines crossing the Arabian Peninsula would allow more of the oil produced in the Persian Gulf to avoid the Strait of Hormuz, according to a new paper from economics professor Dagobert Brito.
   
Discrimination may harm your health
In the study “Is Discrimination an Equal Opportunity Risk? Racial Experiences, Socio-economic Status and Health Status Among Black and White Adults,” sociologists Jennifer Bratter and Bridget Gorman found that approximately 18 percent of blacks and 4 percent of whites reported higher levels of emotional upset and/or physical symptoms due to race-based treatment.
       
 

Making voting more convenient
The convenience of Election Day voting centers can increase voter turnout, according to new research by political scientist Robert Stein. His paper “Voting at Nonprecinct Polling Places: A Review and Research Agenda” looks at existing data on voter identification and nonprecinct voting, two subjects that have generated considerable debate in the media and between political parties.

  
       
 

Looks do matter
People with birthmarks, scars and other facial imperfections are more likely to receive poor ratings in job interviews, according to a new study by psychology professor Mikki Hebl. Findings from her paper “Discrimination Against Facially Stigmatized Applicants in Interviews: An Eye-Tracking and Face-to-Face Investigation” showed that that interviewers recalled less information about these candidates, which negatively impacted evaluations of the applicants.

 
       
 

Social Sciences Alumni Career Panel
The School of Social Sciences convened a career panel for social sciences majors on November 4, 2011. Students attending the panel had the opportunity to ask advice about career and education goals as well as inquire about the long-term prospects of their degrees in the job market.The panel was moderated by Andrea Danburg Kennedy, ’93, and included Dr. Purvez Captain, ’93, Ken Janda, ’77 and Allison Thacker, ’96. All alumni panelists agreed their social sciences degrees from Rice helped pave the way for high-caliber opportunities outside the hedges.
