Designed by Staub and Rather Architects and completed in 1949, what we are pleased to dedicate as Peter and Nancy Huff House was home to five Rice presidents from 1949 until 2004. In the 1990s, the home was named in honor of Ralph S. O’Connor, who established an endowment to ensure ongoing support for the maintenance of the Rice president’s house. In 2006, the house began a new chapter in its history, serving the needs of the university’s current and future alumni. At the time, O’Connor noted that it would be appropriate for the home to one day be renamed in honor of a Rice alumnus or alumna. In 2009, the house was renamed in recognition of Peter ’59 and Nancy Huff, whose generous gift to Rice’s Centennial Campaign made possible significant renovations to the home.
