Volunteer Spotlight

Karen Hess Rogers '68
Rice Historical Society

The Volunteer

In 1994, Karen Hess Rogers met with friends to discuss the possibility of creating a Rice Historical Society "to encourage the study of the institution's history and to create a feeling of community among alumni and friends." A year later, during the summer of 1995, the opening ceremonies took place with Karen as the new president. Now Karen serves as program chair, and continually remains involved in the day-to-day running of the organization, which involves catering, writing, and mailings. Karen says that "what really makes the organization so viable is that it is LOTS of fun, the food is great, and it is a tremendous bargain as well."

Apart from her work with the Rice Historical Society, Karen is a trustee emerita of the Rice Board of Trustees and serves on the Friends of Fondren Library board. She is also involved in the ARA board, Owl Club, and in 1995 was awarded the Meritorious Service Award.

The Organization

The Rice Historical Society is a volunteer organization dedicated to the preservation and promotion of the history of Rice University, and draws on its membership for various tasks. Since its opening, the Rice Historical Society has held numerous lectures, hosted holiday and ranch parties, sponsored seminars, slide presentations, video programs, underwritten numerous digitization projects, taken two trips, and published two books and a booklet as well as a quarterly newsletter, The Cornerstone.

How You Can Get Involved

All of the work of the Rice Historical Society is done by volunteers. To get involved with the organization and to obtain membership information visit http://ricehistoricalsociety.org, call 713-348-4990, or email the RHS.
