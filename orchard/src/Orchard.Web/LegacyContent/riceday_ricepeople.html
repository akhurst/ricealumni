Rice Day 2010: Celebrating Rice People!

Each year the Association of Rice Alumni invites the Rice community on October 12—Rice Day—to help spread the word about Rice by wearing a Rice pin, hat, T-shirt, or however else may suit your personal style.  The idea is that we may be a small university, but with 45,000+ alumni, parents, students, and friends becoming living symbols of Rice, we can make a big impact in a single day to help promote Rice.

For Rice Day 2010, we hope you will be inspired to tell your friends and neighbors about Rice by reading about some interesting Rice alumni, faculty members, and students of whom we can all be proud.


Joe and Ann Hightower '80
Rice professor emeritus of chemical and biomolecular engineering Joe Hightower and his wife of nearly 30 years Ann, an alumna, have followed an extraordinary path and touched thousands of lives along the way as overseers of Houston's Hospitality Apartments. The facility has provided a free place to stay for more than 30 years for people of slender means while being treated at institutions of the Texas Medical Center. Know more.

Anh Reiss '89
Diagnosed with a rare and potentially fatal blood disorder, Reiss's crusade to build up the ranks of bone marrow donors nationwide has earned her a spot on the ballot for People magazine's 2010 Reader's Choice Hero. Know more and vote for Reiss on People's website through October 8.

James Mischka '85
2010 Distinguished Alumni Award recipient James Mischka '85 and his partner Mark Badgley unveiled the 2010 Badgley Mischka line amidst a constellation of fashion's biggest names as part of New York's Fashion Week in September. Badgley Mischka's fall line is here.

Jonathan Cary '08
This former Owls defensive tackle is settling in to life in the "real world" with a position with Deloitte Consulting. Building on what he learned from mentors as a Rice student, he is already making an impact in his community and has his sights set on becoming CEO of a YMCA or Boys & Girls Club. Know more.

Dillon Eng '11 and Josh Ozer '11
What began as a project for a Global Health class became an ongoing effort to extend small loans—rather than charity—to sustainable projects in third-world communities. This morphed into a student club, which is now Owl Mircofinance, and is in the process of obtaining non-profit status.

Jocelyn Brown '10, Joseph Chang '10, Haruka Maruyama '10, Michael Pandya '10, and Katie Schnelle '10
This team of students from the Jones Graduate School of Business developed a "Baby Bubbler"—a portable respiratory support device for infants that can be taken to countries lacking resources for medical equipment. The team subsequently won $11,750 in the prestigious Rice Business Plan Competition, among other honors. Know more.
