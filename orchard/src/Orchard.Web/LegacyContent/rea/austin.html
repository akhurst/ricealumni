The topic:

From the earliest days of the Institute, engineering has been at the forefront of Rice's reputation for excellence.  Join us for an evening of refreshment and inspiration as Dean Ned Thomas explains the pivotal role that engineering played in Rice’s first century, how it will be celebrated at the Rice Centennial, and what the future holds as Rice begins its next hundred years. 

The speaker: 

Edwin L. "Ned" Thomas, the William & Stephanie Sick Dean of Engineering, joined Rice in 2011 having built a distinguished career as a materials scientist and having served for several years as chair of the Department of Materials Science at MIT, the leading program in the nation.  At Rice he holds joint appointments in Mechanical Engineering & Materials Science and in Chemical & Biomolecular Engineering and collaborates with scientists and engineers in the Richard E. Smalley Institute for Nanoscale Science and Technology.

Dean Thomas comes to Rice with high standards and a passion for advancing engineering leadership and student design as integral parts of the undergraduate experience.  In March 2012, his “State of the School” address (presented by REA via live webcast) outlined his specific academic and research goals for the School of Engineering.  Now Dean Thomas addresses the broader topic of the role of engineering in Rice’s past, present, and future.  

Rice Engineering Alumni – Austin:

Rice Engineering Alumni (REA), the alumni organization for the George R. Brown School of Engineering at Rice University, serves as a catalyst for beneficial collaboration and lifelong connections between alumni and the school.  REA pursues this goal by recognizing outstanding achievements, supporting students, and facilitating interactions among alumni, students, faculty and staff.  Launched in 2011, REA Austin is the first regional arm of Rice Engineering Alumni.
