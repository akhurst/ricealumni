Dean of Engineering Calls Alumni to Dialogue
 There is a ripple of excitement flowing through the School of Engineering these days. That excitement is the result of an extended exercise recently begun by Dean Sallie Keller-McNulty. Following the lead of President David Leebron and his Call to Conversation, Dean Keller-McNulty has opened up a similar dialogue for the school of engineering. With the help of Jude Heimel of Jude Heimel and Associates, Dean Keller-McNulty began the process which will, by the time it is completed, involve faculty, staff, students, alumni, and friends of the George R. Brown School of Engineering. The purpose of the process is to formulate a strategic plan to take the school of engineering into the next decades.

The involvement of faculty and staff began in October with Heimel meeting singly with department chairs and administrators. Discussions centered around how things currently work in the departments and what the chairs and administrators perceive as their most pressing challenges.

In November, the dean met with department chairs and institute and center directors to explore important issues related to the school’s core missions of education and research. The day-long off-campus meeting also included representatives from the Development Office and Human Resources and was facilitated by Heimel. Some of the starting points for discussions were strengths and challenges facing department chairs and what expectations they have for their departments in the next three to five years and for the school in the next five to ten years.

"I was impressed by the thoughtfulness of the faculty and the energy they entered these discussions with," Dean Keller-McNulty said. A major topic of this session was the need for a leadership team to see the school through the strategic planning process and to oversee follow through on topics adopted in the plan. This need was filled when the chairs and institute directors agreed to serve as the leadership team.

Since that first meeting, the Dean and Associate Deans Bart Sinclair, Tony Elam, and Janice Bordeaux met in half day sessions with the faculty of each department, and department administrators and coordinators met for two half-day sessions to focus on what they perceived as their challenges, with Heimel facilitating. In these sessions, faculty and staff were asked what they perceived as their departments’ greatest challenges and then asked to come up with possible solutions. The sessions yielded lists of topics to add to the strategic planning document and also resulted in building a sense of community and ownership of the challenges among those participating.

The strategic planning process is now moving into the next phase, when the discussion is opened up to everyone interested in helping shape the future of the school of engineering.

“I encourage every student, every staff member, every faculty member, every alumnus, and the many friends of the school of engineering to join us in this important effort,” Dean Keller McNulty said. “The more input we get, the better this plan will represent the whole of Rice engineering,” she added.

The results of the meetings held so far can be found online at http://www.rice.edu/engineeringourfuture. The website includes a summary of the topics discussed and suggested goals for the school. Each section is followed by a form for submitting comments.

"I want to stress that this document is a work in progress, and as we open this discussion up to the whole body of those who have a stake in the future of the school, changes in the plan will likely follow.”

The Engineering Our Future web site will be open for comments until April 1st. The Dean also will be holding meetings with students, alumni, and others associated with the school to further the discussions on the strategic plan. After the comment period, a draft of the plan will be drawn up and posted online for more comments. When the final document is posted, alumni, students, faculty, and friends of the School of Engineering will be notified.
