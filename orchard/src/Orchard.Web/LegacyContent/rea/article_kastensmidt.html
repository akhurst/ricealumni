Rice Alumnus "Game" Hunting in Brazil
In 1999, Chris Kastensmidt joined Southlogic Studios, a four-person game development studio based in Porto Alegre, Brazil. Under Chris’s direction as Managing Partner, Southlogic Studios became the largest game developer (in terms of revenue) in all of Latin America in 2002. They have since retained their position with hits that include Deer Hunter 2004 and Deer Hunter 2005.

Southlogic Studios uses its own proprietary 3D game development platform known as the Aspen Engine™. This platform supports simultaneous Xbox and PC development and encompasses a wide range of tools and technologies developed by the company. This engine was used in the creation of Deer Hunter 2004 and Trophy Hunter 2003. Southlogic became the first South American company approved for Xbox and Gamecube development.

Before Southlogic Studios, Kastensmidt worked in processor architecture and developer relations at the Intel corporation. He graduated with honors in Electrical and Computer Engineering in 1996 and was chosen the Outstanding Electrical Engineer his Senior year by the REA. He lives in Brazil with his wife and is currently preparing for Southlogic’s next release, Trophy Hunter…coming in Fall of 2006.

Not bad for someone who didn't speak a word of Portuguese until late 1997!
