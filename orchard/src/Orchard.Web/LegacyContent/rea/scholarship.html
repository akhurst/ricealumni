Scholarship Program

Each year REA provides scholarships to outstanding students in the George R. Brown School of Engineering. The REA Student Grants & Awards Committee releases the scholarship application forms in late January, and completed applications are at the end of February.  Further details can be found in the 2011-2012 Scholarship Announcement. 

All scholarships are awarded at the REA/School of Engineering End-of-Year Picnic, at which the entire Rice community is invited to celebrate the achievements of the year.   The 2012 End-of-Year Picnic will be on Saturday April 14th. Over $100,000 in awards and scholarships will be presented. 

Questions regarding the REA Scholarship Program should be directed to the Student Grants & Awards Chair.

Leadership, Research & Service Awards
Beginning in 2011, REA provides four scholarships recognizing excellence in leadership, research, and international service. These scholarships are open to freshmen, sophomores, juniors, and seniors in the School of Engineering.

  • Leadership Excellence Awards – given to two undergraduate engineering students who have shown outstanding leadership at Rice. Applications for the 2011-2012 school year can be found here. 
  • Research Excellence Award – given to an undergraduate engineering student who has performed outstanding academic research at Rice. Applications for the 2011-2012 school year can be found here. 
  • International Service Award – given to an undergraduate engineering student who has distinguished himself or herself through international service. Applications for the 2011-2012 school year can be found here. 

Additional Engineering Awards
The following scholarships from other funding sources are selected by the respective engineering departments and presented by REA at the End-of-Year Picnic:

  • Buckley-Sartwelle Scholarship – created by Jack Boyd Buckley CE ’48 and Helen Sartwelle Buckley BA ’44 in memory of their parents; awarded to an outstanding junior in mechanical engineering. Applications for the 2011-2012 school year can be found here.
  • R.K.M. “Bob” Dickson Prize – created by H. deForest Ralph Jr. BA ’55, ChE ’56 in memory of his roommate Bob Dickson ’55; recognizes an engineering student whose work benefits society. Applications for the 2011-2012 school year can be found here.
  • Hershel M. Rich Outstanding Invention Award – in honor of Hershel Rich EE ’45, ME ’47; recognizes the best engineering invention.
  • James S. Waters Creativity Award – created by an anonymous donor in honor of Professor James Waters EE ’17; recognizes an engineering undergraduate for creative independent work.
  • Ralph Budd Medal – 1935 Commencement speaker Ralph Budd, a civil engineer and railroad executive, donated his honorarium to Rice for the benefit of students.  This award recognizes the outstanding student thesis.
  • Alan Chapman Award – created by Susanne BA ’62 and Mel ME ’61 Glasscock in honor of Professor and Dean Alan Chapman ME ’45; recognizes an outstanding senior in mechanical engineering.
  • Thomas Michael Panos Family Award – created by siblings Michael ME ’52, ’53 and Effie Panos; recognizes a graduating senior who has demonstrated excellence in mechanical engineering.
  • Harrianna Butler Siebenhausen Award – created by C. H. Siebenhausen ME ’50 in honor of his wife Harrianna; recognizes a married engineering student.
  • Franz R. and Frances Brotzen Achievement Award – created by David ME ’58, ’63, PhD ’68 and Patricia Davidson in honor of Professor and Dean Franz Brotzen and his wife Frances; recognizes a mechanical engineering and materials science student who has shown exceptional involvement with the university.

Scholarships at the Science and Engineering Fair of Houston
Through a long-standing partnership, REA provides alumni volunteers who serve as judges and scholarship interviewers at the Science and Engineering Fair of Houston and select two high school students to receive $5000 scholarships to Rice.  The scholarship winners are also invited to be recognized at the School of Engineering End-of-Year Picnic, hosted by REA.   For further details, or to serve as an REA judge and to help select the winners of the Rice scholarships, please visit our Mentoring page.
