Education & Mentoring

Our Focus

Through its Education & Mentoring (E&M) Committee, REA facilitates opportunities for alumni to interact with current Rice students as well as other students in the greater Houston community through programs such as the Engineer Your Career Panel, judging for the Science & Engineering Fair of Houston, and meeting the needs of various volunteer requests that arise throughout the year.

Mentoring Opportunities and Events

Fall 2012

Thu Oct. 4: Research SCREECH Competition
5:00 pm
McMurtry Auditorium, Duncan Hall (Awards Reception starts at 7:30 pm)

The REA is a top level sponsor of the Research SCREECH competition hosted by the Rice Center for Engineering Leadership.  40 engineering graduate students will be given 90 seconds each to deliver a compelling message about their research, highlighting its broader impact to their field and society. This is an excellent opportunity for alumni to interact with current graduate students in the following ways:

1. Sponsorship:

  • For $100, sponsors get their name and logo announced and presented on all of the materials and the keynote at the beginning of the event.
  • For $500, sponsors are given a table and opportunity to give out promotional material during the networking breaks. They also get the above perks, but positioned as a silver sponsor.
  • For $1000, sponsors get the above, but positioned as a gold sponsor.

2. Attendance:

 If you can't sponsor, your attendance is still welcome! Come join us learn about the exciting research happenings at Rice University!

For more information, check out http://rcel.rice.edu/screech. If you have any questions, please contact David Ramirez at dar4@rice.edu

Past Programs

Spring 2012

Mon Feb 20:  Engineer Your Career
7:00 pm
McMurtry Auditorium, Duncan Hall

As part of National Engineers Week, REA, the George R. Brown School of Engineering and Rice’s student engineering groups are co-sponsoring "Engineering Your Career," a panel discussion designed to inform current students and recent alumni about careers open to Rice engineering graduates and how to manage their career paths.  Help mentor today’s engineering majors and network with fellow alums.

Panelists:

  • Justin Singer (Computer Science, President, SMBology) founded the information technology company offering strategic consulting, user experience design, and custom application development.
  • Jim Powers (Mechanical Engineering, ExxonMobil) supports the upstream energy industry both as an engineering expert within the company and as a leader in determining industry-wide standards in oilfield piping.
  • Jeff Ross (Civil Engineering, Rebuild Houston Oversight Committee) served as a senior vice president for an engineering consulting company working in land development, public works, and site development before his committee appointment by the Mayor.
  • Chris Lessing (Bioengineering, Senior Scientist, KCI) conducts research to support product development requirements, regulatory filings, and marketing claims of wound care products. He was part of the first Bioengineering class at Rice.
  • Troy Thacker (Chemical Engineering, CEO, R360) was a founding partner of a $3 billion private equity firm before founding the nation’s leading environmental oilfield waste management provider.

Alumni are invited to join the panelists to mingle with the students over dessert and coffee after the panel.

Please contact Matt Reisdorf, ChE ’98 if you plan to attend the discussion and networking reception following the event.

Fri Mar 2:  Science and Engineering Fair of Houston
George R. Brown Convention Center, Hall D

Through a long-standing partnership, REA provides alumni volunteers who serve as judges and scholarship interviewers at the Science and Engineering Fair of Houston.  In the morning, these volunteers judge the engineering exhibits at the Fair.  In the afternoon, the REA volunteers interview high school juniors interested in engineering fields, selecting two who will receive $5000 scholarships to Rice, contingent on the awardees being accepted at and attending Rice.  The scholarship winners are also invited to be recognized at the REA School of Engineering End-of-Year Picnic, hosted by REA.  

The 2012 SEFH is March 1-3 at the George R. Brown Convention Center.  REA volunteer judging and scholarship interviews will take place on Friday, March 2 in Hall D. 

Parking and lunch for REA judges are covered by REA.  To serve as an REA judge and to help select the winners of the Rice scholarships, please contact Elaine Louie, ChE '06 .

Spotlight:  Opportunities at the Oshman Engineering Design Kitchen, Spring 2012
A principal mentoring focus of REA is to facilitate alumni support of the Oshman Engineering Design Kitchen and its curriculum.  The following message from Dr. Maria Oden, director of the OEDK, summarizes upcoming opportunities for REA members: 

Dear OEDK Friends,

The Oshman Engineering Design Kitchen (OEDK) continues to be an integral part of engineering education at Rice, with much of our achievement due to the invaluable support and involvement of alumni, industry partners, and friends. The OEDK invites you to participate in the following events this spring:

Around the Kitchen Table Discussion -“ Energy and Sustainability
Thursday, March 8, 5:30-7:30 p.m. * OEDK classroom
These events are round table discussions that will highlight ongoing engineering design work of our current undergraduate students. Each evening will focus on a particular theme in engineering. You are invited to come share dinner with the students as they present their design challenge and solutions, and then seek advice about their design projects from seasoned engineers. Between 4-6 design projects will be presented each evening.

2012 Engineering Design Showcase and Poster Session
Thursday, April 12, 4:00-“8:00pm (judges needed 4:30-6:30pm) * Tudor Fieldhouse
The showcase and competition will highlight many of the school’s engineering design projects and represent our students’ success. This is a great opportunity for you to come out and judge the competition while having a chance to witness first-hand the talent and ambitions of our undergraduate engineering students.

3rd Annual Rice Undergraduate Technology Venture Challenge
Thursday, May 3, Times TBD * Jones Graduate School of Business
The Rice Business Collaboration, a student-run entrepreneurial organization, will challenge engineering students to present business plans for the commercialization of their technologies. This event is sponsored by the Rice Alliance, OEDK, and Rice Center for Engineering Leadership.  We seek volunteer judges.

To register for one or more of these opportunities in one simple form, please visit the OEDK Events and Participation page. For additional information or questions, please email Amy Kavalewitz or call (713) 348-8065.

Thank you in advance for supporting engineering design education here at Rice. It is hard to express what a positive influence your participation can have on the students by broadly expanding their education.

Sincerely,

Maria Oden, PhD
Director, Oshman Engineering Design Kitchen

REA Design Team at the OEDK

In January 2012 the REA Board of Directors voted to fund each year, beginning in 2012-2013, a design team at the Oshman Engineering Design Kitchen.  This REA support will allow the OEDK to fund projects that have significant academic merit but which do not naturally lend themselves to corporate support and thus, in the past, may have gone un-funded.
