Bioengineering Department on the Rise
 The receipt of the Whitaker Foundation’s Development Award, Special Opportunity Award, and Special Award has allowed the Bioengineering Department to experience growth in reputation and presence at Rice. Since these awards, the department has redoubled efforts to integrate research and education. Total research expenditures for the department have doubled in the last three years, reaching nearly $6 million in 2005.

This fall, the Bioengineering program was named to the list of the top 10 programs in America by US News and World Report in its 2006 ratings of national graduate programs. To remain competitive, the department is advancing its research efforts by increasing the number of proposals submitted; continuing to recruit high-quality, recognized faculty and staff members; growing our student population; and strengthening our ties to the business community through partnerships with the Texas Medical Center, and industry and government relations.

In addition, the Bioengineering undergraduate program has developed and published an integrated engineering program. Bioengineering Fundamentals, written by Drs. Ann Saterbak, Ka-Yiu San, and Larry McIntire (Georgia Tech), is slated for publication this summer by Prentice Hall. The textbook covers the conservation laws of mass, energy, charge and momentum with applications in biological and medical systems. The textbook is rich with worked examples and many homework problems. Case studies on the heart, lungs and kidneys integrate the application of the conservation laws. Already used by several leading departments across the country, the textbook provides a new and unifying approach for the interdisciplinary, sophomore-level course.
