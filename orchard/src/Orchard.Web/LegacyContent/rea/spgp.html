Student Project Grant Program

In 2011 REA, with assistance from the Engineering Development Office, initiated a program to support outstanding engineering related student-led initiatives at Rice.  This program is structured to provide financial support to engineering related student-led activities which are not supported by traditional academic funding sources and which are not directly related to class work.

The minimum grant amount is $500 and the maximum grant amount is $1500.  The REA will award between $2500 and $4000 in grants during the Fall Grant Cycle and between $2500 and $4000 in grants during the Spring Grant Cycle.  Grant winners are required to provide a presentation to the REA Board of Directors upon completion of the project and are recognized at the REA/School of Engineering End-of-Year Picnic in April. 

Yearly Schedule for Grant Program

The Fall Grant Cycle supports projects which will be started, completed, or have significant progress during the Spring Semester.  The Spring Grant Cycle supports projects which will be started, completed, or have significant progress during the summer and Fall Semester. 

Fall Grant Cycle Schedule:

  • Applications due to Engineering Development Office in mid-November
  • REA to announce Grant Winners in mid-December

Spring Grant Cycle Schedule:

  • Applications due to Engineering Development Office in mid-February
  • REA to announce Grant Winners in mid-March

Application Process
Applications are to be delivered by email to Angela Young, Executive Director of Engineering Development, prior to the announced application deadline. Exact application timeline and detailed specifications regarding application content and formatting can be found here.

Grant Selection & Award Process
The REA Student Awards Committee will select award winners based on the following four criteria:  (1) demonstrated need for financial support; (2) educational impact for involved students; (3) societal impact of proposed project; and (4) feasibility of project.

Previous Grant Recipients

Spring 2012

  • Rice Center for Engineering Leadership Graduate Committee, for a graduate student Research Rally Competition
  • Rice Business Collaborative Entrepreneurship Division, for the 3rd Annual Rice Undergraduate Venture Challenge

Fall 2011

  • Rice chapter of Engineers Without Borders, for its Nicaragua III project.
  • Rice Solar Car Team, for its project to build and race a solar-powered vehicle. 

Questions?
Questions regarding the REA Student Project Grant Program should be directed to the Student Awards Chair.
