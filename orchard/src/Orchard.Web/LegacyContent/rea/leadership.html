Board of Directors Meetings

The REA Board of Directors holds four principal meetings per year on the Rice campus, and holds shorter meetings of the Board or its executive committee roughly once per month. The business of the Board is transacted at these meetings.

The four principal Board meetings require in person attendance, while the monthly meetings may be attended in person or by teleconference. All Directors are expected to attend the Board’s Kickoff Meeting in August; to attend at least two (preferably all three) of the other principal meetings; and to participate in the monthly meetings as required.

Applications / Nominations to serve on the REA Board

To fulfill its mission, REA seeks dedicated Rice engineering graduates to serve on the REA Board of Directors. 

The REA Board of Directors is not an advisory or honorary body, but a working board. Directors need not reside in Houston, but are expected to attend a few keynote REA functions in Houston.  Directors are further expected to:

  • support REA at one of the sponsorship levels
  • regularly attend Board meetings in person or electronically
  • serve faithfully on multiple REA committees
  • attend principal REA events
  • represent REA at other university and professional events
  • engage actively with faculty, students, alumni and friends of the School of Engineering; and
  • contribute valuably to the work and mission of REA. 

An REA Board member can expect to devote 50-75 hours per year to Board work and REA activities.

Selection to serve on the REA Board is competitive.  Experience in management, non-profit leadership or Rice alumni programs is desired.  All candidates/nominees for the REA Board are required to submit an application to the Board Development Committee.  The Board Development Committee accepts applications continuously; reviews applications in January through March of each year; and presents its recommendations to the REA Board in April.  Selected applicants are notified in May, attend the June Board meeting as observers, and begin their terms on July 1.

REA strives to ensure that a diversity of majors, professions and class years are represented in the application process and on the Board. 

To request an application, submit a nomination, or for further information, please contact the Board Development Chair.
