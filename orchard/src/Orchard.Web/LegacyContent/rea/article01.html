Outstanding Engineering Alumni Awards

The REA annually recognizes outstanding engineering alumni in two categories. The Outstanding Engineering Alumnus award has been presented every year since 1974. It recognizes alumni for careers of outstanding professional achievement and community service. The Outstanding Young Engineering Alumnus award was presented for the first time in 1996. It recognizes the achievements of engineers under the age of 40.

Nominations are currently being accepted for the 2006 Outstanding Engineering Alumnus/a and Outstanding Young Engineering Alumnus/a award. Please contact Elaine Lange '76 for more information.


2005 Outstanding Engineering Alumnus:
Pat H. Moore '52

Pat Moore received his B.S. in civil engineering from Rice in 1953 and has done graduate and post-graduate work at the University of Houston and Tulane. He served as President of Fluor Ocean Services, Inc. from 1968-80 and Executive Vice President of Raymond International, Inc. from 1980-86, when he then formed his own international consulting company, Martin Moore, Inc. In 1990, he closed his business to become Executive Vice President for Finance and Administration for Brown and Root, Inc.

While building a highly successful business and engineering career, Pat found the time to contribute to a myriad of other professional and community activities including Director of the Houston World Trade Association, member of the Houston Chamber of Commerce and the Forum Club of Houston, Founding Director of the Texas Commerce Bank, Director of the Meyerland Community Improvement Association, and Deacon of his church.

Pat served on the Rice University Board of Governors from 1984-88. He was also Chair of the Rice University Fund Council and Chair of the George R. Brown School of Engineering Advisory Council. Pat continues to teach a course on ethics at Rice as an Adjunct Professor in Civil and Environmental Engineering.

2005 Outstanding Young Engineering Alumnus:
Timothy R. Dehne '87

Tim Dehne, senior vice president of R&D, leads the global R&D organization for National Instruments in Austin. His responsibilities include product strategy, product development, and product quality.

In his current role, Dehne manages R&D centers in the United States, Germany, China, and India. Under his direction, NI R&D has developed and adopted global strategies, processes, and metrics for successfully innovating quality products that lead to worldwide customer success. NI engineers work together to design, build, and test virtual instrumentation software and hardware to become part of a broad platform of computer-based solutions with unmatched integration. During his 16 years at National Instruments, Dehne has served as NI vice president of engineering, NI vice president of marketing, NI vice president of strategic marketing, and NI GPIB product marketing manager.

Dehne is currently a member of the Dwight Look College of Engineering External Advisory Council at Texas A&M University and serves on the Editorial Advisory Board for R&D Magazine.
