GIVE TO RICE

 


You are part of an exclusive group of alumni invited to attend this special gathering. Involved as a student or active as an alum, you are invited to lead the way as Rice continues on the extraordinary journey charted by its founders.

Hear about cutting-edge research and the changing academic landscape. Mingle with deans, vice presidents, and other university leaders; get an insider’s view and share ideas. Visit with students about Rice today. Connect with fellow alumni leaders and discover the many ways to be involved.

You were a student for only a short time, but you are an alum for life. The journey toward excellence — guided by the unbounded imagination and ambition of our predecessors — is a lifetime pursuit. Join us and lead.

Registration has ended. Please contact the alumni office at 713-348-4057 if you wish to attend or if you have registered and your plans change.

Dear Rice alumni leader,

The history of any great institution is one of great leadership. When Edgar Odell Lovett began his founding efforts at Rice in 1908, Houston was a modest-sized city of only 80,000 people. Education in the South seemed almost an afterthought: the average child attended school for less than twelve weeks a year. Fewer than two percent of college-age youth attended college. Yet as Lovett stood on the prairie on the far southwestern fringes of Houston — a mile beyond the nearest paved road — he saw not what was, but what could be.

As Rice continues to evolve and enhance its efforts, its leaders are again called to consider the Rice that could be. Rice’s seventh president, David W. Leebron, has extended this leadership challenge to the entire Rice community — including alumni — with his “Call to Conversation,” a document that invites alumni to discuss Rice’s goals and priorities for the years to come.

The Association of Rice Alumni invites you to join in the conversation and engage President Leebron and other key campus decision-makers as an attendee of the 2005 Conference for Alumni Leaders. During the weekend, you’ll have the opportunity to learn about the workings of the university, share insights with fellow alumni leaders and Rice administrators, and get involved in shaping Rice’s future. We hope you will join us for this unique event.

Harry Gee ’60, Conference Chair
