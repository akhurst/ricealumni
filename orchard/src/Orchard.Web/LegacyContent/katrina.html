Hurricane Katrina relief assistance

The Rice community is mobilizing to assist our neighbors affected by the devastation of Hurricane Katrina. As Houston begins to receive refugees from the storm, Rice alumni can help with donations of time, money, food, and shelter. Below is a list of campus, local, and national relief efforts.

Campus efforts (Houston area)
Web links and instructions for the campus community to assist with relief efforts. Site includes information with regard to monetary donations, donations of goods, and volunteer service and training.
   

Volunteer Houston
Lists volunteer needs and training information for shelters and organizations across Houston.

   

Houston Food Bank
Accepting monetary and food donations.
   

American Red Cross
Online monetary donation form for Katrina relief.
   
MSNBC "Looking For" and "Safe" lists
International Red Cross Family News Network
Registries to help locate friends and family members in the path of the storm.
   
HurricaneHousing.org
Shelter for Katrina
Web forums offering a centralized and organized way for people to ask for and offer shelter for Katrina victims.
   

Conference USA relief efforts
The conference has established a C-USA Relief Fund to assist those affected and relocated to Hurricane Katrina.
   

Houston-area Tulane students
In support of a sister school stricken by Hurricane Katrina, Rice University will offer Houston-area undergraduates enrolled at Tulane University the opportunity to take fall semester classes at Rice on a space-available basis.
